## The Essence of Thai Massage

Thai massage is often called "lazy yoga" because the therapist guides your body through a series of yoga-like stretches. But there's nothing lazy about the results:

- **Deep muscle relief**: Targeted pressure releases chronic tension
- **Improved flexibility**: Gentle stretching increases range of motion
- **Energy flow**: Working along sen lines promotes vitality
- **Mental clarity**: The meditative rhythm calms the mind

## What Makes Thai Massage Unique

Unlike oil-based massages, traditional Thai massage is performed fully clothed on a comfortable mat. The therapist uses hands, thumbs, elbows, knees, and feet to apply pressure and guide stretches. This full-body approach creates a deeply therapeutic experience that addresses both physical and energetic imbalances.

## History of Thai Massage

Thai massage, known locally as "Nuad Thai," has a history spanning over 2,500 years. It was developed by Jivaka Kumar Bhaccha, a physician to the Buddha, who combined elements of yoga, Ayurveda, and traditional Chinese medicine.

### Traditional Techniques

The core of Thai massage involves:
- **Sen Lines**: Working along the body's energy pathways
- **Stretching**: Yoga-like movements to improve flexibility
- **Pressure Points**: Targeted pressure to release tension
- **Rhythmic Compression**: Using palms, thumbs, and elbows
